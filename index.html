The Physics Behind CASE

THE SCIENCE

CASE is built on three pillars of cutting-edge theoretical physics — each validated in laboratory conditions, now engineered to planetary scale.

⚛
Matter-Antimatter Annihilation

When a proton meets an antiproton, both are entirely converted to energy — predominantly pions and gamma rays. This is the most energy-dense reaction known to physics, releasing 100% of the rest mass as energy.

E = mc² → 0.24g × c² = 2.4 × 10¹⁵ J
🧲
Penning Trap Containment

Antihydrogen cannot touch ordinary matter or it annihilates instantly. A Penning trap uses magnetic and electric fields to suspend antihydrogen atoms in vacuum — CERN first achieved this in 2010. CASE scales this to 0.24 grams.

B-field: ~5 Tesla // Vacuum: 10⁻¹⁰ mbar
🚀
Hall-Effect Ion Propulsion

Ion thrusters accelerate xenon ions using electric fields, achieving specific impulse 10× better than chemical rockets. 48 hall-effect thrusters in array give CASE the delta-v budget to reach 12 million km intercept points.

Isp: 3,000s // ΔV: ~15 km/s capability
🎯
Quantum-Enhanced Guidance

Quantum inertial sensors using cold-atom interferometry achieve acceleration sensitivity 1,000× better than classical systems. Combined with onboard radar ranging, CASE achieves ±0.3m CEP at 12 million km.

CEP: ±0.3 m @ 12,000,000 km
💥
Asteroid Fragmentation Physics

A 2.4 petajoule pulse delivers ~10 megajoules per kilogram to a 240,000-tonne asteroid — exceeding the binding energy by 4 orders of magnitude. The body does not deflect; it vaporizes and disperses as plasma.

2.4 PJ ≈ 573 kt TNT equivalent
🔭
Early Warning Network

Singularity's 24-telescope network, combined with CASE's onboard phased-array radar, can detect a 50m object at 50 million km — giving up to 20 years of warning for Earth-crosser asteroids on collision trajectories.

Detection range: 50M km // Resolution: 50 m
